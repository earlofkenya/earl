# Earl

## Overview

I build data systems, cloud workflows, and applied AI tooling with an emphasis on clarity, reliability, and real operational use.

My work sits at the intersection of data engineering, analytics, and machine learning. A large part of it is focused on turning complex, messy, or fragmented data into structures that are easier to trust, query, automate, and extend.

I am especially interested in:

- data engineering and platform design
- cloud-native pipelines and automation
- SQL- and Python-based transformation systems
- applied AI, retrieval, and LLM workflows
- healthcare, biomedical, and research-oriented data problems

## What I Work On

### Data engineering

I design and maintain data workflows that move beyond one-off scripts and become durable systems. That includes cohort-building logic, transformation layers, governed topic pipelines, and operational SQL that can be reviewed, versioned, and rerun with confidence.

### Cloud and platform work

My background includes building and operating cloud-based data and integration workflows across Azure and GCP, with an emphasis on maintainability, reproducibility, and practical delivery.

### Applied AI

I work on retrieval, language-model, and multimodal workflows where the objective is not novelty for its own sake, but useful systems: better extraction, stronger context handling, clearer interfaces, and outputs that are actually usable downstream.

### Domain-heavy data problems

I am drawn to projects where the data model matters as much as the tooling. That includes environments where lineage, quality, interpretation, and operational constraints all have to be handled carefully rather than assumed away.

## How I Like to Build

I prefer systems that are:

- explicit in their logic
- easy to review and reason about
- resilient to bad or inconsistent data
- operationally simple to rerun
- documented well enough that another engineer can take them over without guesswork

I care less about fashionable architecture than about whether the system is understandable, correct, and sustainable.

## Working Style

My default approach is to make technical work more legible. That usually means:

- reducing ambiguity in data definitions
- separating core logic from incidental complexity
- building with reproducibility in mind
- writing SQL and Python that can survive real operational use
- preferring strong foundations over fragile shortcuts
